01 — Strategic Analysis: Meta's Partner Model vs. Ours
Should We Copy Meta's Top-10 Partner Approach?
Meta limits SDK publishing to "select partners" during preview, with general availability in 2026. They curate a small number of high-visibility integrations (Twitch, Disney, Microsoft Seeing AI, 18Birdies). The question is whether this curated approach or an open marketplace generates more value for Sensory Bridges.

Meta's Approach: Curated Select Partners

Limited SDK preview → select partners only → GA 2026
How it works: Meta handpicks partners. Twitch, Disney, Microsoft Seeing AI, 18Birdies, Logitech Streamlabs were early access. Each solves a different use case. Partners must apply and be accepted.

Why they do it: Hardware quality control (bad apps drain battery on $799 glasses), brand protection (Meta curates the experience), and creating showcase demos that sell the platform to other developers.

Revenue model: Meta does NOT charge partners for SDK access. Meta sells glasses hardware. Partners drive use cases that sell more glasses. Meta wins on volume, not marketplace fees.

NE Low Vision example: Training company (not a partner with Meta directly) offering $150+ personalized setup sessions for Ray-Ban Meta glasses. They created a service business on top of Meta's hardware without Meta's permission or involvement — pure ecosystem value.
→

Recommendation for Sensory Bridges: Hybrid Model

Curated clinical partners (Year 1) → Open marketplace (Year 2+)
Year 1 (2026–2027): Curated. Handpick 5–8 clinical partners who extend the platform's value. Each fills a specific gap in the enterprise system. You control the experience, protect IP, and build showcase integrations that sell the platform to school districts.

Year 2+ (2028+): Open marketplace. Publish partner API documentation. Revenue share model: Sensory Bridges takes 15–20% of partner subscription revenue generated through the platform. Partners get distribution to your installed base. You get ecosystem lock-in and passive revenue.

Why this is better than pure Meta-style: Meta can afford to give away the SDK because they make margin on $799 hardware. You can't — your hardware is Samsung's at Phase 1. Your margin is in the platform subscription. Partners that add value to the platform increase willingness-to-pay for the subscription tier, AND generate direct revenue share income.

The NE Low Vision lesson: Someone will build training services around your product whether you partner with them or not. Better to formalize it, take a revenue share, and control the quality.
Sources: Meta Wearables Device Access Toolkit FAQ (Dec 2025) · Meta Connect 2025 partner announcements · NE Low Vision training program page · Omdia: "Meta's multi-tiered AI glasses launch aims to capture 80% of market" (2025) · Canalys SaaS 100 Ecosystems: "Leading platforms like Salesforce have 75% partner-driven revenue"

SLP AI Software Partner
Clinical Integration
Language sample analysis engine (e.g., InnerVoice / Lois Brady). Processes voice metadata from BrooksClip. Auto-generates SLP evaluation reports.
Revenue share: 20% of partner sub to Sensory Bridges. Partner charges $29–$49/mo per clinician.

How they plug in:

Integration point: BrooksClip voice activity metadata (timestamps, duration, amplitude contour) piped via Bridges API to partner's analysis engine. No raw audio transmitted. Partner returns structured language assessment data that populates the IEP documentation platform. Requires: Partner-executed DPA + FERPA addendum.

Source: Lois Brady consultation Mar 24, 2026. InnerVoice AI language analysis platform.
🎮
OT/PT Game Content
Content Partner
Licensed therapeutic game challenges for VoiceGrow avatar. Bilateral coordination, deep breathing sequences, motor planning activities.
Revenue share: 70/30 split (partner/Sensory Bridges). Content priced at $2.99–$9.99 per challenge pack.

How they plug in:

Integration point: VoiceGrow Game Engine API. Partner submits challenge content via Bridges Developer Portal. Content is reviewed for clinical appropriateness by advisory board. Rendered on watch, tablet, or AR glasses. XP earned from challenges flows into the regulation data pipeline.

Source: OT/PT skill-building design from VoiceGrow system architecture (Mar 24, 2026).
🧘
Calming Content Provider
Content Partner
Licensed calming music, guided breathing, sensory regulation audio. Delivered through watch speaker or AR glasses audio. OT-curated playlists.
Revenue share: 70/30 split. Subscription add-on at $4.99/mo per user.

How they plug in:

Integration point: Feedback Layer API. Content triggered by FSM state transitions — when system enters Alert state, calming content plays. Stimulus rotation logic ensures the same content is never played twice consecutively (anti-contamination safeguard per Patent E). Partner provides content library; Sensory Bridges controls delivery logic.

Source: Reinforcer contamination safeguard architecture (Prov E). Lois Brady clinical consultation.
📊
IEP Platform Integration
Data Partner
Bidirectional sync with existing IEP management systems (Frontline/PowerSchool, Goalbook). Auto-populate progress data from wearable sessions.
Enterprise licensing: $5K–$15K/yr per integration partner. Or per-district fee of $500/yr.

How they plug in:

Integration point: Documentation Layer REST API. Brooks Band session data (regulation events, time-in-range, IEP goal progress) formatted to partner's data schema via FHIR-compatible export. Partner's platform displays Brooks Band data alongside other IEP documentation. FERPA consent gate applies — data only flows when parent has authorized the specific integration.

Source: IDEA § 300.320 documentation requirements. Frontline/PowerSchool feature set analysis.

AR/VR Therapy Platform
Hardware + Content
Immersive social regulation environments. Avatar transfers from watch to VR. Partner provides VR therapy content; Sensory Bridges provides regulation data layer.
Revenue share: 15% of partner subscription. Partner charges $39–$79/mo for VR therapy access.

How they plug in:

Integration point: Avatar Transfer Protocol (Prov F). VoiceGrow avatar state, XP, and regulation profile exported to partner's VR environment. Child's avatar appears in social skills scenarios calibrated by their real-world regulation data. Requires Ray-Ban Meta Display ($799) or Meta Quest. Meta Wearables Device Access Toolkit SDK for glasses integration. GA publishing expected 2026.

Sources: Meta Wearables Developer FAQ (Dec 2025). Floreo VR ($40/mo autism social skills). Prov F claims.
📱
Parent Companion App
Service Partner
Consumer-facing parent dashboard and coaching. Weekly regulation summaries, milestone celebrations, therapist communication portal.
Included in consumer subscription ($149–$249/yr). Partner develops under contract or revenue share.

How they plug in:

Integration point: Analytics Layer API. Aggregate session data (no raw biometrics) presented in parent-friendly format. VoiceGrow avatar stage visible to parent. IEP goal progress displayed in non-clinical language. Push notifications for milestones ("Brooks stayed in range 80% of the day today!"). COPPA compliance required for any child-linked data.

Source: Sensory Bridges consumer pricing model. COPPA 16 CFR Part 312.
🏥
Medicaid Billing Intermediary
Service Partner
School-based Medicaid billing services. Partner handles claims submission using documentation auto-generated by Brooks Band platform.
Per-claim fee to district (not to Sensory Bridges). Platform value: increases billable sessions → increases platform stickiness.

How they plug in:

Integration point: Documentation Layer export API. Session notes, CPT code-aligned records, and HCBS waiver-formatted documentation exported in partner's required format. Partner submits claims to TennCare MCOs on behalf of the district. Sensory Bridges does NOT bill Medicaid directly and does NOT touch claims money. Value: platform generates the documentation that enables billing, making it indispensable to districts.

Sources: TennCare Billing Manual (Sep 2025). CMS SBS Guide (Mar 2024). CMS TAC deadline Jul 1, 2026.
📚
Training & Certification
Service Partner
Professional development for SLPs, OTs, and educators on Brooks Band deployment. Certification program. (NE Low Vision model applied to assistive tech.)
Revenue share: 20% of training fees to Sensory Bridges. Partner charges $150–$500 per training session.

How they plug in:

Integration point: Not a technical API integration — this is a service partnership. Partner provides personalized setup, configuration training, and ongoing professional development. Modeled after NE Low Vision's Ray-Ban Meta training program ($150+ per session). Sensory Bridges provides training materials, certification curriculum, and approved partner listing on website. Creates an ecosystem of certified deployment specialists who sell the platform for you.

Source: NE Low Vision Ray-Ban Meta training program (nelowvision.com). Meta partner ecosystem model.
